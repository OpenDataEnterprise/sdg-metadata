  <h1>Methodology</h1>
2
  <p>At a country level, the contribution of sustainable marine capture fisheries to the GDP is calculated as follows:</p>
3
  <p>a) The percentage contribution of fisheries and aquaculture to GDP is estimated by simply dividing the value added of fisheries and aquaculture by national GDP.</p>
4
+ <p><em>GDP from Fisheries and Aquaculture (%) = Value Added Fisheries and Aquaculture/GDP</em></p>
5
+ <p><em>GDP<sub>FIA</sub> = VA<sub>FIA</sub>/GDP</em></p>
6
+ <p>b) In order to disaggregate for the value added of marine capture fisheries and the value added of aquaculture, the quantity of fish produced from marine capture fisheries will be divided by total quantity of national production of fish, and then multiplied by the percentage of GDP from fisheries and aquaculture. As such, the quantity of production of marine capture fisheries is used as a proxy for the value of marine capture fisheries.</p>
7
+ <p><em>Value added of Marine Capture Fisheries proxy (%) = </em></p>
8
+ <p><em>GDP from Fisheries and Aquaculture x (Quantity of Marine Capture Fisheries/Total Quantity of Fish)</em></p>
9
+ <p><em>VA<sub>F </sub>= GDP<sub>FIA</sub> x (Q<sub>M</sub>/Q<sub>T</sub>)</em></p>
10
  <p>c) The sustainability multiplier will be calculated based on the average sustainability published periodically for each FAO major marine fishing area. </p>
11
  <p>For each country, the sustainability multiplier will be the average sustainability weighted by the proportion of the quantity of marine capture for each respective fishing area in which the country performs fishing activities. When a country fishes in only one FAO fishing area, its sustainability multiplier will be equal to the average sustainability of stocks in that area. </p>
12
+ <p>Sustainability multiplier = Sum of (Sustainability for Each region x ((Quantity fished from Each marine region/Total Quantity fished from All marine regions))</p>
